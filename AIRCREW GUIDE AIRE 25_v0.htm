WELCOME ADDRESS

 

Dear colleagues, dear aviators, dear friends:

On behalf of the Spanish Air and Space Chief of Staff, as Commander of San Javier Air Base, home of the Air and Space Force Academy, receive my warmest welcome to this unique location nearby the Mar Menor.

Welcome to “Aire-25”, the greatest airshow to celebrate the 40th anniversary of the Spanish acrobatic team “Patrulla Águila”. An incredible four decades have flown by since a group of courageous fighter pilots created this team in 1985 and started to perform airshows all over the world and became Spanish ambassadors painting the skies with our national flag. During this special weekend it’s time to recognize these heroes and share with them a remarkable celebration accompanied with other sister acrobatic teams, many aviators and general public from everywhere.

 This “Aire-25” airshow has been organized by the Spanish Air and Space Force, as a whole, with the support of the surrounding towns of San Javier, San Pedro del Pinatar, Los Alcázares and also with the support of local and regional authorities, and it will be seen by half a million people around the beaches of Mar Menor. It’s going to be a blast!!

Along with this Display Order, we have enclosed all the necessary information and procedures needed to safely and efficiently participate in “Aire-25”.  If you have any further questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to contact your designated POC.

I look forward to saluting you in person, wishing you a successful and safe Aire-25 airshow and a joyful stay in this beautiful Murcia region.

 

Col. Luis Felipe González Asenjo

Spanish Air Force and Space Academy Director

SAFETY RULES FOR AIR DISPLAY

 

General rules

• Aerobatic flying in clouds within the display air traffic zone is prohibited.

• Mach number 0.95/600 Kts. shall not be exceeded.

• Crossover maneuvers by fixed wing A/C towards spectators enclosure are prohibited except as specifically authorized by the Display Director.

• Engine/s are not to be shut down during flying demonstrations.

• Aircraft are not to be flown outside the aircraft’s proven limitations.

• Aircraft are not to fly over the Spectator Enclosures unless specifically authorized by the DD.

• No manoeuver is to be attempted that is likely to jeopardize the safety of spectators in the event of mishap or misjudgment.

• Multi-engine aircraft are not to fly below the speed at which it is possible to climb away, without change of configuration, should any engine fail to respond to an acceleration demand.

• Only manoeuvers or sequences which have been accepted and passed by the DD are to be performed.

• No use of flares is allowed during the Display.

VIOLATION OF REGULATIONS

 

Yellow Card

Aircrew violating rules stated above during display/rehearsal shall receive a warning directly via radio or at the debriefing.

 

 

Red Card

Repeated or serious violation will result in an immediate landing, and may jeopardize the participation in future events.

EMERGENCIES

Each pilot will take all appropriate actions to handle the aircraft emergency and will notify the DD as soon as possible, stating the nature of emergency and the assistance required.

Should the emergency lead to possible runway closure, pilots will be requested, if possible, to divert to the alternate airfield.

HOT BRAKES

 

In case of suspected hydrazine contamination and/or hot brakes, the pilot will inform the DD and he will be directed to park the aircraft on the dedicated spot, waiting for Fire Fighters assistance.

                RADIO FAILURE

 

If a radio failure condition occurs during display, the pilot must immediately interrupt the aerobatic program, perform a low pass over the runway rocking the wings and perform a landing for the runway in use.

If radio contact is lost in the holding area, attempt to contact San Javier ATC using any published frequency. In the event of a total R/T failure, depart the holding avoiding the display area and proceed to diversion, carrying out standard R/T failure procedures.

                JETTISON/FUEL DUMP AREAS

 

Fuel dump is only authorized in case of emergency. Fly over the Mediterranean Sea above 5000 ft.

Perform jettison at 1000ft avoiding populated areas. Recommended area: Northwest of El Carmolí (200/5).

 

                EJECTION AREA

 

In case of a controlled ejection, if situation permits, proceed to 200/5nm (Northwest of El Carmolí – Small conical mountain). Try to steer your aircraft to the sea or to any unpopulated area before the ejection.
